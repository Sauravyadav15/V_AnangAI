KINGSTON ATTRACTIONS DATABASE - WITH ACCESSIBILITY INFORMATION
================================================================

ACCESSIBILITY LEGEND:
- Full Access: Fully wheelchair accessible throughout
- Partial Access: Main areas accessible, some limitations
- Limited Access: Significant accessibility barriers
- Accessible with Assistance: Accessible with help/advance notice
- GN Washrooms: Gender-neutral washrooms available
- Accessible Washrooms: Wheelchair-accessible washrooms available
- Contact Venue: Information not publicly available, contact directly

Place Name: Fort Henry
Location: 1 Fort Henry Dr.
Location URL: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Fort+Henry,+1+Fort+Henry+Dr,+Kingston,+ON
About: A massive 19th-century British military fortress featuring dramatic sunset ceremonies and daily soldier drills. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site offering stunning views of Lake Ontario and the city.
Hours: Daily: 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM (May–Sept)
Fees: ~$20.00 - $25.00
Accessibility: Partial Access - Historic buildings vary in accessibility. Main floors of most buildings accessible, some via alternative entrances. Complimentary wheelchairs available at ticket area.
Washrooms: Accessible washrooms available

Place Name: Kingston Penitentiary
Location: 560 King St. W
Location URL: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kingston+Penitentiary,+560+King+St+W,+Kingston,+ON
About: Canada's oldest and most notorious maximum-security prison, now open for guided tours that explore its architecture and history. Tours provide a chilling and fascinating look into the lives of both inmates and guards.
Hours: Daily: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (May–Oct)
Fees: $42.13 - $81.13
Accessibility: Wheelchair accessible
Washrooms: Accessible washrooms available


Place Name: 1000 Islands Cruises
Location: 248 Ontario St.
Location URL: https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=1000+Islands+Cruises,+248+Ontario+St,+Kingston,+ON
About: Relaxing sightseeing and dinner cruises that weave through the world-famous Thousand Islands. It is the best way to see the region's waterfront estates, lush greenery, and historic shipwrecks.
